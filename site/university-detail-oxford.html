Admissions & Selection

Why University of Oxford?

Oxford's world-leading academic standing is driven by its tutorial system, where students meet weekly with professors in groups of one to three. The collegiate structure (being a member of both the University and a specific College) provides close-knit academic and social communities.

Admissions timeline

UCAS applications for Oxford close early — typically by **October 15** of the year preceding entry. Key selection filters include:

  • Academic Grades: Exceptional grade requirements (typically AAA/A*A*A at A-Level, or 38-40 points at IB).
  • Admissions Tests: Required subject-specific tests (e.g., TSA for PPE/Economics, PAT for Physics, LNAT for Law) sat in late October/early November.
  • Written Work: Submission of academic essays for relevant humanities subjects.
  • Interviews: Rigorous academic interviews conducted in December by college tutors.

Admissions advice

Oxford tutors seek deep academic potential and "teachability." Preparation should look beyond basic school curricula: read extensively outside your syllabus, practice talking through complex academic problems aloud, and master the exact format of the subject-specific entrance tests.

FAQ

Common questions.

When do I need to apply to Oxford via UCAS?

The UCAS deadline for Oxford (and Cambridge, as well as most medical courses) is significantly earlier than other universities, typically around October 15th of the year before entry.

Can I apply to both Oxford and Cambridge in the same year?

No, you can only apply to either Oxford or Cambridge in a single admissions cycle for undergraduate courses.

How important is the admissions interview?

The interview is a critical component of the Oxford admissions process. It's designed to mimic an Oxford tutorial and assess your critical thinking, academic passion, and ability to engage with complex new ideas in your subject area.
