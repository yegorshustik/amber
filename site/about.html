About · Non Aurum Sed Nomen

Not Gold, But the Name.

We are named for the trade that built Gdańsk — but what we protect cannot be weighed or sold.

The name

Carved into a merchant's house.

"Non Aurum Sed Nomen" — Not Gold, But the Name — is carved into the facade of one of Gdańsk's oldest merchant houses. For centuries it guided this city's traders: that reputation outlasts riches, and trust is the only currency that compounds.

We built Amber Council on that same principle. Every family who comes to us carries something more valuable than a transaction: a name, a future, a story they are trying to write under difficult circumstances.

Our mission

Our work is not to process applications. It is to guard your child's name as carefully as a merchant once guarded his own.

Gold can be spent. A name has to be earned — and kept.

Founder

Yegor Shustik

After navigating the complexities of international education and elite admissions, I realized that families don't need another agency pushing partner schools. They need an independent strategist who tells them the truth, manages the entire timeline, and treats their child's future with the same rigor as a critical investment.

Amber Council was built to be exactly that: an advisory firm where discretion, honesty, and long-term results are the only metrics that matter.

What this means

Four principles we don't bend.

Advice in your interest

We're not paid to fill seats at any particular school or university — we're trusted to find the right one.

Discretion as a discipline

Visa records, financial documents, family circumstances — handled the way a name, once given, cannot be taken back carelessly.

Relationships, not transactions

We measure success not in applications submitted, but in families who return — year after year, school after school, country after country.

A name worth carrying

Our goal is not simply admission — it's helping each student build a name of their own, one that opens doors long after we've stepped back.

Who we work with

Families who refuse to lower the bar.

We work with families in the Tri-City — Gdańsk, Sopot, Gdynia — and Polish families abroad, at the moment the stakes are highest: when state school ends and the next decade is decided.

They want one thing above all: someone genuinely accountable for their child's path — honest about what is realistic, and present the whole way through.

FAQ

Honest answers.

Are you independent, or do schools pay you?

We work for the family, not for any institution. We are not paid commissions to place students at particular schools or universities — our only incentive is to find the right fit.

What does "honest targeting" mean?

We tell you what is realistically reachable for your child — ability-weighted, not what sells. If a goal isn't realistic, we say so, and map the strongest path that is.

What exactly are you accountable for?

Every stage and every deadline. We own the process end-to-end and stay reachable 24/7 throughout — nothing falls through.
