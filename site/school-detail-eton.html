Admissions & Preparation

Why Eton College?

Eton offers a unique combination of historical traditions, exceptional academic instruction, and vast extracurricular opportunities. It remains one of the largest feeder schools to Oxford, Cambridge, and top US Ivy League universities.

Admissions timeline

Admission is highly selective and begins years in advance. The key entry point is Year 9 (age 13). The process involves two primary stages:

  • Stage 1 (Year 6, age 10-11): The Eton List Test (ISEB Common Pre-Test), focusing on verbal and non-verbal reasoning, English, and Mathematics, plus a headmaster's report.
  • Stage 2 (Year 6/7, age 11): The Eton College assessment, consisting of an individual interview and a computerized test of academic potential.

Admissions preparation

To successfully navigate Eton's entrance requirements, we recommend starting academic planning no later than Grade 4. Preparation should focus on verbal reasoning mastery, critical thinking, mock interview practice, and broad extracurricular engagement to develop character.
